& so on

treble clef dollar sign — a fashi0ncrime$ collage of sound in the blurred space between signal and static. Loops stutter, textures warp, voices glitch and ghost, splintering across four tracks—part artifact, part memory trace. Nothing is fixed. Nothing is clean. Each piece leans into distortion, letting the noise tell its story. Formed in the static of early 2000s Brooklyn—dial-up heard through screen-flickered windows, busted Casios, and the afterglow of a fading DIY scene—treble clef dollar sign is memory glitching in real time. Sound wedged in the space between archive and dissonance: lush pads dissolve into blown-out drums, autotuned murmurs warp into digital shrieks and soft melodies with frayed seams. Equal parts overheard and deeply internal, shaped by internet wormholes, a voyeuristic glare into the soul of chain store brain rot, and the eerie pulse of obsolete tech. What emerges is unformed, cinematic, and shape shifting—a mind map etched by vapor trails and digital noise.

make

Written and recorded over a string of late nights, treble clef dollar sign took shape—layered and degraded loops, salvaged drum machine patterns, vocal takes recorded through broken gear. It started as a test of old sounds pushed through newer machines—morphing into something intuitive and charged. With no fixed plan, just a sense of chasing texture: the hiss of a bad cable, the crunch of over-compression, voices bending under pitch. Tracks built from fragments with nowhere else to go. Knowing and yet not knowing when to stop—when the track felt like it was about to fall apart but still held something real. treble clef dollar sign sits in a room lit by a single monitor glow, looping through memory, distortion, and the urge to keep going even when the signal fades.
